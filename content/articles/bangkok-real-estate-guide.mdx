# A Complete Guide to Bangkok Real Estate: Where to Buy, Rent, and Invest

Bangkok, the vibrant capital of Thailand, offers one of Asia's most dynamic real estate markets. Whether you're looking to buy your dream home, invest in rental properties, or find the perfect rental, this comprehensive guide will help you navigate Bangkok's property landscape.

## Current Market Overview

Bangkok's real estate market has shown remarkable resilience, with property prices appreciating steadily over the past decade. The city's population growth, economic development, and status as a regional hub continue to drive demand for quality housing.

### Key Statistics
- **Average Price per Sq.M.**: ฿150,000 - ฿300,000
- **Annual Appreciation**: 3-5%
- **Rental Yield**: 4-6% gross
- **Population Growth**: 1.5% annually

## Best Neighborhoods for Different Lifestyles

### For Families: Sukhumvit & Phrom Phong
These established neighborhoods offer excellent schools, parks, and family-friendly amenities. Properties here range from modern condominiums to traditional houses.

**Pros:**
- Excellent infrastructure
- International schools nearby
- Green spaces and parks
- Good public transportation

**Cons:**
- Higher price point
- Traffic congestion during peak hours

### For Young Professionals: Sathorn & Silom
The central business district offers modern condos with easy access to offices, shopping, and entertainment.

**Pros:**
- Walking distance to BTS/MRT stations
- Vibrant nightlife and dining
- Modern amenities
- High rental demand

**Cons:**
- Higher maintenance fees
- Limited parking

### For Budget-Conscious Buyers: Bang Sue & Don Muang
These emerging areas offer more affordable options with good growth potential.

**Pros:**
- Lower entry prices
- Future development potential
- Good connectivity via new BTS extensions
- Mix of residential and commercial spaces

## Property Types and Their Benefits

### Condominiums (Condos)
The most popular choice for Bangkok residents, especially expats and young professionals.

**Advantages:**
- Modern facilities (pool, gym, security)

- Rental income potential

### Townhouses
Perfect for families seeking more space and privacy.

**Advantages:**
- More living space

### Single Houses
Traditional Thai homes or modern villas in quieter areas.

**Advantages:**
- Maximum privacy

- Potential for expansion
- Cultural authenticity

## Investment Opportunities

### Rental Properties
Bangkok's rental market is robust, especially for quality condos in central locations.

**Target Areas:**
- Sukhumvit Road

Investing in properties under construction can offer:
- Lower entry prices

### Commercial Properties
For experienced investors, commercial spaces in growing areas can provide higher returns.

## Buying Process in Thailand

### Step 1: Research and Planning
- Define your budget and requirements
- Research neighborhoods thoroughly
- Consult with local real estate agents
- Consider future developments and infrastructure projects

### Step 2: Finding Properties
- Work with licensed real estate agents
- Use online platforms and property websites
- Attend property exhibitions and launches
- Network with expat communities

### Step 3: Due Diligence
- Verify property ownership (Chanote)
- Check building permits and approvals
- Hire a property inspector
- Review HOA rules and fees (for condos)

### Step 4: Making an Offer
- Submit a letter of intent
- Negotiate terms with seller
- Pay booking deposit (typically 10-20%)

### Step 5: Legal Process
- Hire a lawyer experienced in Thai property law
- Conduct title search
- Sign sales and purchase agreement
- Transfer ownership at Land Department

## Legal Considerations for Foreign Buyers

### Ownership Restrictions
Foreigners cannot own land directly in Thailand, but can own condominiums under certain conditions.

### Leasehold vs. Freehold
- **Freehold**: Full ownership rights
- **Leasehold**: Long-term lease (usually 30-99 years)

### Taxes and Fees
- Transfer fee: 2% of property value
- Stamp duty: 0.75% of property value
- Registration fee: 1% of property value
- Withholding tax: 1% for foreigners

import fs from "fs";
import path from "path";
import matter from "gray-matter";

export interface Article {
  slug: string;
  title: string;
  description: string;
  date: string;
  author: string;
  image: string;
  tags: string[];
  content: string;
}

const articlesDirectory = path.join(process.cwd(), "content/articles");

export function getAllArticles(): Article[] {
  const fileNames = fs.readdirSync(articlesDirectory);
  const articles = fileNames
    .filter((fileName) => fileName.endsWith(".mdx"))
    .map((fileName) => {
      const slug = fileName.replace(/\.mdx$/, "");
      const fullPath = path.join(articlesDirectory, fileName);
      const fileContents = fs.readFileSync(fullPath, "utf8");
      const { data, content } = matter(fileContents);

      return {
        slug,
        title: data.title,
        description: data.description,
        date: data.date,
        author: data.author,
        image: data.image,
        tags: data.tags || [],
        content,
      };
    })
    .sort((a, b) => new Date(b.date).getTime() - new Date(a.date).getTime());

  return articles;
}

export function getArticleBySlug(slug: string): Article | null {
  try {
    const fullPath = path.join(articlesDirectory, `${slug}.mdx`);
    const fileContents = fs.readFileSync(fullPath, "utf8");
    const { data, content } = matter(fileContents);

    return {
      slug,
      title: data.title,
      description: data.description,
      date: data.date,
      author: data.author,
      image: data.image,
      tags: data.tags || [],
      content,
    };
  } catch (error) {
    return null;
  }
}

export function getArticlesByTag(tag: string): Article[] {
  const articles = getAllArticles();
  return articles.filter((article) =>
    article.tags.some((t) => t.toLowerCase() === tag.toLowerCase())
  );
}
